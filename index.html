The Man Behind the Movement

He Did Not Wait for Power to Begin Serving. He Served First. Now Power Must Follow Purpose.

Born in Iju-Odo in the heart of Okitipupa Local Government Area of Ondo State, Bamidele Omosehin carries the DNA of his community in every decision he makes. He is the Traditional Prime Minister — the Lisa — of the ancient and venerable Osooro Kingdom, a title that speaks not of self-aggrandisement, but of duty. A title that demands humility before authority. A title that says: I exist to lift my people.

As a businessman and entrepreneur who built his fortune through diligence and vision, he could have chosen the comfortable life — far from the dusty roads of Okitipupa, far from the challenges of Irele. But he chose otherwise. He chose to come home. He chose to invest in the people who shaped him. And in doing so, he became not just a philanthropist — he became a movement.

Through the Bamidele Omosehin Foundation (BOF) and the Dele Omosehin Development Initiative (DODI), he has funded scholarships, supported widows, backed community health outreaches, hosted youth empowerment programmes, partnered with skill-acquisition initiatives for women, and organized sports competitions that give young people in Ikale land a reason to dream bigger. None of this was done for votes. It was done because he believed it was right.

The Asejere Blueprint

Four Pillars of Transformation for Okitipupa and Irele

Asejere is not a slogan. It is a structured vision built on four unshakeable foundations, each already proven through Chief Omosehin's years of service before seeking any office.

🎓

Education & Youth Empowerment

From scholarships to vocational training, Chief Omosehin has invested in the minds and hands of the next generation. His Foundation has covered fees for students who would have dropped out, and equipped young people with skills to build thriving businesses. In Abuja, he will advocate for federal allocations that reach every school in Okitipupa and Irele.

👩‍👧

Women & Community Development

He has described women as "the soul of strong communities." Through partnerships like the Bimbeads Concepts jewelry-making programme, he has opened doors of economic independence for women and girls. He believes: No constituency truly develops until its women are empowered.

⚽

Sports, Culture & Youth Engagement

Six editions of the BOF Unity Cup have brought communities together, discovered talents, and given restless youth a productive outlet. Sport is nation-building. In Abuja, Chief Omosehin will push for federal sports infrastructure investment in Ondo South and youth talent development programmes.

🏗️

Infrastructure & Healthcare Access

The road from Ore to Igbokoda. A Federal Medical Centre for Okitipupa. Bitumen exploration that can power an economic renaissance. These are not dreams — they are blueprints waiting for a voice in Abuja who knows this land and loves its people. Chief Omosehin will be that voice.

A Track Record of Service

He Has Been Serving Long Before He Was Asking for Your Vote

This is not a man who discovered his people during election season. Every milestone below happened before any ballot, before any campaign podium.

🏛️

Founding of Bamidele Omosehin Foundation (BOF)

Established to provide educational support, social welfare, and community development to the indigent residents of Okitipupa, Irele, and beyond — funded entirely from his own resources, without any political office or government support.

🎁

Annual Empowerment — Over 2,000 Beneficiaries Per Year

Every year, Chief Omosehin distributes food items, cash gifts, and working tools to widows, the elderly, youth, and vulnerable groups across Ikale land. In December 2025 alone, over 1,000 underprivileged residents received support — all funded personally.

⚽

BOF Unity Cup — 6 Editions of Peace and Talent Discovery

The annual football competition has become a beloved institution in Okitipupa, uniting communities, discovering sporting talent, and providing youth with a platform for positive expression. Royal fathers, LGA chairmen, and APC executives attend in solidarity every year.

👩‍💼

Women's Skills Acquisition — Bimbeads Partnership

In partnership with Bimbeads Concepts, Chief Omosehin launched a programme training women and girls across Okitipupa and Irele in jewelry-making, mentorship, and market access — giving them sustainable livelihoods without charity dependency.

👑

Official Declaration — The Asejere Movement Unveiled

After extensive consultations with party leaders, traditional rulers, women groups, youth leaders, and community stakeholders across Okitipupa and Irele, HRH Dele Omosehin officially declared his candidacy and launched the Asejere Movement — peace, prosperity, and people-centered leadership.

🤝

COYCO & Multiple Endorsements — Grassroots Groundswell

The Coalition of Youth and Community Organizations (COYCO) unanimously endorsed Chief Omosehin, pledging to mobilize support across all six LGAs of Ondo South. Their message: "Imagine what he could achieve with a bigger platform to serve."

The Case for Omosehin

We Do Not Need Another Politician. We Need a Statesman.

Here is the truth that every citizen of Okitipupa and Irele must reckon with: Nigeria has no shortage of politicians. What it lacks — what our constituency has hungered for, generation after generation — is a leader who serves before seeking. A leader whose record of impact precedes any campaign slogan. A leader whose love for his people is written not in speeches, but in scholarships paid, in bellies fed, in women empowered, in youth engaged, in communities rebuilt.

Chief Bamidele Omosehin is that leader.

He understands that the role of a representative is not merely to travel to Abuja and collect sitting allowances. It is to carry the voice of every farmer in Irele, every widow in Okitipupa, every young graduate who cannot find work, every mother whose child sits under a leaking classroom roof — to carry those voices into the chamber of power and refuse to sit down until those voices are heard.
